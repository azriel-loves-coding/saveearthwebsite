About Azriel

Hi, my name is Azriel. I am a Year 1 student in Singapore Polytechnic(SP) studying in Common Infocomn Technology, also known as Common ICT. I came to SP via PFP, so this is actually my 2nd year in SP. Well, this is some background information about me. I'll be talking about my values, interests and personality in the sections below :)

Values

1. Loyalty

“Loyalty is a two-way street. If I'm asking for it from you, then you are getting it from me.”
Quoted from one of my favourite fictional characters: Harvey Specter from the TV series Suits, meaning that loyalty comes from both sides and is not just one-sided. In order to earn loyalty from others, you have to earn it by showing it and not just expecting and demanding it. I value loyalty very much as friends come and go in life, but the real ones stay, and the ones who stayed are the ones who have been there for me when I wasn't the brightest person in the world and helped me throughout my darkest times to help me develop in life, whether it's in improving my character or helping me achieve my goals, and are happy to see me break the glass ceiling. In comparison to some friends I had before, they were only friends with me to hang out and have fun and only call me when they need something but when I want to do something for myself, they comment that I'm a "tryhard" and don't support me in achieving my goals and leave when things get tough. This was what made me realise that real and loyal friends are rare and that I should treasure them as much as possible. Therefore this is why loyalty is one of my values.

2. Humility

"Humility to the end."
This is a quote I came up with on my own, with inspiration from the quote "Honor, to the end" by Optimus Prime from the 4th Transformers movie: "Transformers : Age of Extinction" which means that you should do things in a honourable manner throughout your life as winning isn't everything but how you do it matters as well. But that's on honor and what Optimus Prime's quote meant, my quote on humility means that no matter how good you become at something, you should always remain humble and not let your ego get to you as it's a big world out there and there's plenty to learn in life, and you can always strive for greater heights. I apply humility to my life when I'm working towards something, whether it's an academic goal, a goal for an exercise in the gym, a coding project I'm working on or doing a jigsaw puzzle. I do so by taking consistent steps towards achieving my goals, and avoid getting cocky and start slacking off when I reach a big milestone during the journey towards my goal. Like the story "Slow and Steady wins the race" where the toitoise beat the hare in a race as the hare got too cocky and slept when he's nearing the finish line while the toitoise continue moving slowly towards it, showing that I should never get too cocky and start slacking off once I've become really good at something as it'll cost me bigger achievements and affect how I'll treat others who aren't as good as me but are trying to achieve similar goals. Instead, I should remain humble and continue learning and move on to achieve higher goals, and never look down on others but respect them for the work they put in as I was once in their position. Therefore, this is why humility is one of my values as it's crucial to have as a human being and to become successful.

3. Self-reliance

"The strongest man in the world is he who stands alone."
Quoted by Henrik Ibsen, a Norwegian playwright and theatre director during the 18s. This quote means that a man who only relies on himself has a very strong and hard to break will. Self-reliance is one of my values as in life, the only person you have is yourself. Sure, you can ask others for help and point you in the right direction. But at the end of the day, it is you who's doing the work, no one can do it for you. Therefore, I believe that it is important to be comfortable being by yourself as people won't be there for you all the time and there will be times when all you have is yourself and that is when you will have to be independent and self-reliant. Hence, this is why I value self-reliance.

Interests

1. Reading Batman comics

"All men have limits. They learn what they are and learn not to exceed them. I ignore mine"
Quoted by the caped crusader, the dark knight and the bat of Gotham himself: The Batman. Batman is my favourite DC hero and one that I look up to as he is just a human being but is able to take down powerful characters, he even has a contingency plan to immobilise the entire Justice League if they ever gone rogue. This shows that Batman is a strategic thinker and a really good one as he can think of all possible outcomes and prepares for it, which is also shown in the various gadgets and tech he has at his disposal. Another reason why I admire Batman is his strong will and determination to protect Gotham City. The quote above is from the Batman comic: "Batman: Knightfall" where Bane broke out all the Arkham inmates and let them terrorise Gotham to mentally and physically exhaust Batman so that Bane will be able to break Batman easily. In spite of the face that Batman knew that someone was behind the Arkham breakout to mentally and physically exhaust him, he still went all over Gotham to stop the Arkham villains as he cares more about protecting his city and it's people than his well-being, showing how committed he is to his mission to protect Gotham City and its citizens and not letting anything or anyone stand in his way of doing so, even himself. This is also reflected on the fact that he can go for 80 hours without sleep, which he does on a regular basis to watch over his city at night and solve crimes. But this is just Batman's character, I can't just know about him as a character and call myself a Batman fan. No, I have to know his stories as well, which is why I read Batman comics to learn about his stories and to earn the title "A true Batman fan" in my book as I believe that in order to be a true fan of a character, you can't just watch the movies or TV series but read the comics as well as comics are where the origins of a character are from. Thus, I started reading Batman comics this year and I'm still reading them until now and I'm enjoying every second of it.
